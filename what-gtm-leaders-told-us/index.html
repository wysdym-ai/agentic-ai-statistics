What 25+ GTM leaders told us about AI agents — and the data behind it

We've spent the last stretch in conversation with go-to-market leaders — RevOps, sales, and marketing operators at mid-market and enterprise teams — about what actually happens when you put AI agents to work. Five themes came up again and again. Here they are, each paired with the public research that backs it up.

How to read this. These are qualitative themes from 25+ conversations, not a quantified survey — we're not reporting percentages from the interviews, because we didn't run one. What we are reporting is what we heard, alongside third-party public data that corroborates it. Every statistic below is external research, attributed and linked to its source.

"We keep adding agents. Nothing compounds."

The agents work in isolation and demo well — but each one starts from scratch, and the stack as a whole never gets smarter.

Nearly every leader was already running multiple agents, added quarter over quarter. The frustration wasn't that any single agent failed — it's that the collective didn't improve. Work done by one agent never made the next one better.

The data behind it
  • 66.4% of agentic AI is already multi-agent — most teams aren't running one agent, they're running many. — Landbase / Market.us, 2025
  • MCP installs went from ~100K to ~97M in 16 months — the connective plumbing exploded, but shared context didn't come with it. — Anthropic, 2025

"The model was never the problem. The data underneath it was."

Pilots didn't die because the model reasoned badly. They died because the agent stood on stale, scattered, or ungrounded knowledge.

Leaders who had run failed pilots almost never blamed model quality. They described agents citing a competitor they'd stopped losing to, or a pain point resolved two quarters ago — failures of foundation, not intelligence.

The data behind it
  • 95% of enterprise GenAI pilots return zero — largely because the systems don't retain context or learn. — MIT Project NANDA, 2025
  • Grounding a model in a structured knowledge graph moved enterprise-question accuracy from 16% to 54% — same model, better foundation. — data.world benchmark
  • 60% of AI projects will be abandoned through 2026 without AI-ready data. — Gartner

"I can't put an ungoverned agent near my CRM."

Trust is the adoption gate. A suggestion is fine; an agent writing to the system of record unsupervised is a non-starter.

The blocker leaders named most wasn't capability — it was governance. Without human-in-the-loop, an audit trail, and a way to gate consequential writes, agents stay stuck in read-only demos and never touch real pipeline.

The data behind it
  • Over 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by end of 2027 — largely on cost, unclear value, and inadequate risk controls. — Gartner, 2025
  • Roughly 3 in 10 teams don't evaluate their agents at all — no way to trust, so no way to scale. — LangChain

"We're paying for the same work over and over."

Every agent re-wires the same integrations and re-pays for the same retrieval. The duplication is quiet, but it adds up.

When the same connectors and the same context get built separately into every agent, teams pay for the same lookup a dozen times. The cost isn't the model — it's the redundancy around it.

The data behind it
  • LLM inference costs fell 90%+ in about two years — yet duplicated retrieval across isolated agents quietly erases much of that saving. — Stanford HAI, 2025
  • The AI orchestration market is projected to grow from $11B to $82B (~22% CAGR) through 2035 — spend is shifting to the coordination layer. — Precedence Research, 2026

"What we actually need is a shared foundation — not another agent."

Don't sell me a tenth agent. Give me the layer underneath the ones I already have, so they finally work together.

The clearest signal across every conversation: leaders are past the point of wanting more agents. They want the substrate — shared memory, skills, governance, and a feedback loop — that turns a pile of agents into something that compounds. It's the "system of record → system of intelligence" shift, made real for the agent era.

The data behind it
  • Only 16% of enterprise AI deployments qualify as true agents that plan, act, observe, and adapt — the rest need a foundation to become that. — Menlo Ventures
