What you told us

The two things slowing AI down inside Vena

You named them directly: nobody can safely let an agent write to the system of record, and nobody can attribute what AI actually did to revenue. Those aren't agent problems. They're harness problems — and they're the two we obsess over.

Write-access governance

Agents that can only read are toys; agents that can write are a risk. Today that trade-off has no safe middle. So the writes stay manual.

Outcome observability

Token dashboards tell you cost, not contribution. You can't see which AI action advanced which deal stage — so AI stays a cost line, not a revenue line.

Memory that doesn't carry

Every agent re-learns Vena's context from scratch. Nothing the team approves, edits, or wins teaches the next agent anything.

The harness

Five components, mapped to your stack

Wysdym is the platform every GTM agent runs on — not another agent. It sits under whatever Vena already uses and makes all of it grounded, governed, and accountable.

wysdymGateway — one governed door Live

Read-only by default. Every agent write to the CRM hits a per-agent RBAC + approval queue with a full audit trail. Your team approves; the harness remembers the decision. The safe middle you didn't have.

Attributes AI actions to deal stages, not tokens. Findings flag drift and deal-risk with the exact write to fix it. AI becomes a line you can defend to your CFO.
