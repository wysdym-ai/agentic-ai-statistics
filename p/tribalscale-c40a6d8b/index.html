What you told us

Why a DIY GTM agent doesn't hold

You already pay for the adjacent AI GTM tools — Pulse, ARA — and you stood up your own agent on top. The problem isn't the agent. It's that it decays without constant upkeep, it has no shared brain to draw on, and you want outcomes, not an "agent in a box." Those are substrate problems. They're the three we obsess over.

The agent decays without upkeep

A DIY agent is only as fresh as the last time you hand-fed it. Skills go stale, context drifts, and the upkeep tax never ends. Nothing makes it learn on its own.

No shared brain to plug into

Your agents each carry their own slice of context. There's no per-tenant memory underneath them that every agent inherits — so nothing compounds across the stack.

You want outcomes, not a box

An "agent in a box" demos well and proves nothing. You want actions tied to results — what actually moved a deal — not another dashboard of tokens and latency.
